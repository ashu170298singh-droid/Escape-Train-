Prologue · Platform Zero · 11:47 PM
🌃
The Briefing
A stormy evening. A sealed envelope. A train that never stops.

The station clock reads 11:47 PM. Platform Zero. You stand alone in the rain, overcoat soaked, briefcase in hand.

A courier in a dark coat materialises from the crowd, presses a sealed envelope into your hands, and vanishes before you can speak. Inside, a single card in gold ink:

"Six carriages. Six cases. Each holds a bias that has quietly derailed a career, closed a door, or erased an opportunity. Your lens is the only thing that can set the record straight. Board now. The train will not wait."

The Lens Express hisses at the platform edge, its amber windows cutting through the dark like a string of lanterns. You step aboard.

🎙 Intercom"Welcome, Investigator. Six biases. Six carriages. The truth is never obvious — that is precisely why the world needs investigators like you. Carriage One awaits."
🚂
The Lens Express departs into the night…

Carriage One · The Interview Car
👔
The Polished Candidate
First impressions are powerful. Are they always accurate?

The carriage is styled as a corporate interview room — leather chairs, glass desk, two framed photographs mounted side by side. A brass placard reads: CASE FILE 001.

You open the dossier. "Two candidates applied for a senior engineering role. Their qualifications are identical in every measurable way. The hiring manager made her decision within 30 seconds — before reading a single word of their CVs."

Candidate A: sharp suit, confident professional headshot. Candidate B: casual clothes, natural photo taken outdoors. Equal portfolios. Equal experience. One job. One bias.

👔🤝👕
Same qualifications. Different first impressions.
🔍
Case File 001 · Identify the Bias
Which bias caused the hiring manager to prefer Candidate A before reviewing any qualifications?

Carriage Two · The Mentorship Car
🪞
The Mirror Effect
We elevate those who reflect our own story back at us.

This carriage is warmer — walls lined with decades of framed mentor–mentee photographs, each pair beaming. A dossier rests on the velvet seat. CASE FILE 002.

You read: "A senior manager must nominate one analyst for the company's high-potential leadership programme. Two analysts have near-identical performance records. She nominates Priya — who attended the same university, shares the same sport, and communicates in the same way the manager herself did ten years ago."

The other analyst, Marcus — equally qualified, equally performing — receives no nomination, no feedback, no explanation. Six months later, he leaves the company.

🕵️ Manager"She genuinely deserves this. She reminds me so much of myself when I was starting out."
🎙 NarratorWell-intentioned. Quietly devastating for Marcus. This is how bias hides in plain sight.
🔍
Case File 002 · Identify the Bias
What unconscious bias is shaping the manager's nomination?

🚂 Evidence Logged — Key Findings
◆First Impression Bias causes snap judgements based on appearance — before a single qualification is reviewed.
◆Affinity Bias leads us to champion those who mirror our own story — often silently erasing equally deserving others.
◆The Halo Effect allows one brilliant moment to over-inflate ratings across unrelated competencies.
◆In-group Bias makes us excuse our own group's failures as bad luck while blaming other groups' failures as personal flaws.
◆Proximity Bias systematically disadvantages remote workers whose results are equal but whose presence is not.
◆Blindspots and stereotypes persist because they hide in plain sight. Naming them is the first act of change.
